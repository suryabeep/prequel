Case Study

About the Client

The client is amongst the top 10 global automobile companies and is the world’s largest manufacturer of motorcycles and internal combustion engines with a market cap of $48 billion as of Sep 2020. The Indian subsidiary of the client is India’s 2nd largest 2-wheeler manufacturer with a customer base in excess of 46 million and has 4 manufacturing units with a production capacity of 6.5 million units/annum

Business Problem

The client has ambitious digitalization plans for its Indian subsidiary. The company’s leadership is keen on leveraging new-age technologies like artificial intelligence (AI), Machine learning (ML) & big data in planning function and towards creating data/data science-driven decision-making frameworks.

The company was previously using spreadsheets to plan operations and had dedicated teams for planning optimization across different functions (5-6 mid-level planning resources per function). But to truly implement the digital agenda, they needed to shift towards a scalable and agile solution that could understand their environment, rules/constraints and thereby create optimized planning in sync with them.

As dispatch planning was an area where the client was facing problems due to complexities in the planning environment leading to higher freight cost & lower sales fulfillment, they wanted to test the efficacy of AI solutions in the dispatch domain. These complexities were:

  • Multiple variables: 250 SKUs (models, variants, colors), 1000+ dealers, 8 stock points (plant and regional W/H), 14-day dispatches.
  • Environmental constraints: limitations in transportation (fixed carrier capacity, order interchange rules for safety stock) and scheduling (FTLs/LTLs, stock availability).
  • Uncertainties: internal process linked (payment probability, sales order changes), external environment linked (weather, political, operational etc.).
  • Processes: Payment clearance, stock transfers.

There were three objective criteria for optimizing:

  1. 100% order fulfillment
  2. Minimum freight cost
  3. Even spread of dispatches through the planning cycle

Solution

Iterative data modeling coupled with propriety self-learning AI algorithms of VERDIS delivered an optimized dispatch plan.
Multi-stage simulations generated best-fit plans (mapping 150 million possible nodes) adhering to boundaries and constraints of the sales requirement (OTIF).

Results

The VERDIS solution was observed and compared with the manual planning team’s output over six dispatch planning cycles (Jan to March 20) & the following emerged:

  • Savings of 16.5% freight cost/unit
  • More than 80% reduction in time and effort to prepare dispatch plan
  • 100% stock allocation for dispatch for sales fulfillment
  • Extraneous uncertainties were incorporated/factored in planning.

Current Status

The dispatch planning is now being done entirely by VERDIS. The planning output is validated by 1 resource. The dispatch planning module’s success has resulted in the client starting to use VERDIS for production planning as well.

The CXO dashboards and BI tools work in a very intuitive, easy to use manner and MIS, reports & BI is being delivered over e-mail to the CXO’s across functions
